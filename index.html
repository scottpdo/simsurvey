Simulation Survey Results

What's going on here?

This is a visualization of the results of a 3-question survey on computer simulations. The goal is to gain some insight into how people think about computer simulations. This is not a very scientific survey, nor is it IRB-approved! So, that's just to say — take this all with a shaker of salt. Anyway, the survey asks:

  1. What is a simulation?
  2. Is it possible for a simulation to precisely and accurately model reality?
  3. Do you believe we are living in a simulation?

Findings

As of December 2, 2017, 60 people have taken the survey. Of those:

  • About half (51.7%) of the respondents answered between 6 and 8 on the ability of simulations to model reality.
  • About half (53.3%) of the respondents think there is a 0% chance that we are living in a simulation.
    • (Which also means that about half think there is a non-zero chance (!))
  • A strong contingent (10%) of the respondents think that we are absolutely not living in a simulation and that it is also impossible for a simulation to precisely and accurately model reality.
  • Only 1 person is absolutely certain that we are living in a simulation. Their definition: "Our current reality."
  • More analysis of the respondents' definitions of 'simulation' forthcoming...
